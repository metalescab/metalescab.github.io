Dr. Julio César Azcárate, an accomplished chemist, earned his Ph.D. from the Universidad Nacional de La Plata. Specializing in nanomaterials, he is an Associate Researcher at CONICET and the Bariloche Atomic Center. His research focuses on the synthesis and characterization of nanoparticles for catalytic and plasmonic applications, aiming to improve chemical reactions' efficiency and explore light manipulation at the nanoscale.

Julio's dedication extends beyond research; he is a mentor and educator, co-advising doctoral theses and teaching Transmission Electron Microscopy at the Instituto Balseiro. His commitment to guiding the next generation of scientists underscores his passion for education.

Active in the scientific community, Julio regularly presents at conferences, sharing his findings and collaborating to advance nanomaterial science. His work is published in numerous high-impact journals, reflecting the significance of his contributions.

Julio's career exemplifies dedication to chemistry, with his research, mentorship, and teaching driving innovation and discovery in nanomaterials.
